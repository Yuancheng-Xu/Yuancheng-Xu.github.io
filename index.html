About me

Yuancheng Xu (徐源诚) is a PhD student at the University of Maryland, College Park advised by Prof. Furong Huang. He obtained the bachelor's degree in mathematics from Southern University of Science and Technology. Before joining UMD, he had worked with Prof. Christina Ramirez on developing machine learning algorithms for high dimensional longitudinal data with correlated feature at UCLA, and worked with Prof. Sukbin Lim on computational Neuroscience at NYU Shanghai.

Research
Exploring and Exploiting Decision Boundary Dynamics for Adversarial Robustness
Yuancheng Xu, Yanchao Sun, Micah Goldblum, Tom Goldstein, Furong Huang
International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR), 2023
  • Study the relative speed of the decision boundary in the input space w.r.t. any data point during training, by deriving a closed-form formula for the speed.
  • Derive an analytic expression for the margin's gradient and propose a new robust training method that directly increases margins by following the gradient and prioritizes increasing smaller margins.

FREEtree: A Tree-based Approach for High Dimensional Longitudinal Data With Correlated Features
Yuancheng Xu, Athanasse Zafirov, R. Michael Alvarez, Dan Kojis, Min Tan, Christina M. Ramirez
Preprint, 2020
  • Designing FREEtree which reduces the bias for feature selection when features are correlated by leveraging clustering methods and Linear Mixed-Effect Model Tree.
  • Adapting Weighted Correlation Network Analysis (WGCNA) to longitudinal dataset by using distance measures for time series such as dynamic time warping (DTW).
